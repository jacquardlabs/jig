| # | Lane | Failure mode | Detection hint |
|---|------|--------------|----------------|
| 1 | technical | Load-bearing false negative: a `Rests on:` line that describes its dependency without literally naming the task ("builds on the groundwork above") makes the Foreman's text scan miss the edge, so a genuinely load-bearing task ships with no Inspector — the exact "nothing signs off on itself" gap this story exists to close, silently reintroduced for that one task even though its skip note is stated. | In the required demo, compare the Foreman's stated load-bearing set (computed right after Step 1.4) against the PLAN.md's actual dependency intent; grep the skip notes for any task whose downstream dependent's `Rests on:` prose is non-literal, then confirm the stated skip reason ("no other task's Rests on: names it") makes the miss debuggable rather than invisible. |
| 2 | technical | The Inspector's commit-range scoping is off by one and leaks an adjacent task's diff. The design scopes it to "this task's first dispatch through its final verify-passed commit (spanning FIX/RESAMPLE)"; if the range includes task N-1's tail or task N+1's head, the Inspector reviews the wrong change and its CLEAR/DEFECT/CONCERN verdict is unsound. | Inspect the actual dispatch prompt captured for the load-bearing task in the demo; confirm the `git diff`/`git show` range names only this task's own commits, spanning its re-dispatches but nothing before its first dispatch or after its verify-passed commit. |
| 3 | technical | The load-bearing derivation lives in Foreman procedure (an LLM re-reading `Rests on:` prose), not a script, so it is non-deterministic — two runs of the same PLAN.md could compute different load-bearing sets, meaning whether a task is inspected at all depends on run-to-run LLM variance rather than a fixed property of the plan. | Run `/build` twice against the same hand-written two-task PLAN.md; diff the two stated load-bearing sets. Any divergence is the failure. |
| 4 | technical | The second-DEFECT recheck (an open question in the doc) is implemented as an unbounded Inspector re-dispatch: each DEFECT triggers a fresh Inspector that DEFECTs again, and the failure routine never reaches the Foreman's REPLAN-vs-ESCALATE diagnosis. | Read the shipped step 2.6 failure-routine wiring in SKILL.md; confirm a second DEFECT on the same item ID reaches a bounded terminal step (a single independent recheck, then Foreman REPLAN/ESCALATE), not open recursion — mirroring the existing "second FAIL" step's bound. |
| 5 | product | Jurisdiction quietly widens in implementation: a build executor writing the step 2.6 prose adds a fourth "reasonable-sounding" lens (a light security, style, or performance check), duplicating what `/gate-audit` already owns and violating issue #15's narrow-jurisdiction framing. | Diff the shipped step 2.6 prose against the three named lenses (test self-dealing, contract match, technicality gaming); any inspection dimension beyond those three is the finding. |
| 6 | product | A DEFECT or CONCERN is reported bare — just the token, without the triggering lens and the Inspector's cited reasoning — leaving the human unable to tell which of the three lenses fired or why, defeating the "never bare"/"none silent" posture the design commits to for both verdicts and for the leaf-skip note. | Exercise the DEFECT and CONCERN branches in the demo; confirm each report names, inline at the moment it fires, which lens triggered and the Inspector's cited detail — not only a token plus a report file the human must go hunting for. |
| 7 | product | CONCERN forwarding depends entirely on a later `/gate-audit` auditor reading the committed evidence file; nothing actively routes the concern to its named lane. If the auditor's "review the full changeset" pass does not surface the task's evidence directory, the concern is silently dropped and the forward never happens. | Confirm the CONCERN report path sits inside the branch diff `/gate-audit` reads (committed as part of step 2.7's evidence directory) and is self-describing — it names its own lens and reasoning, so a diligent auditor cannot miss what it is even without out-of-band routing. |
